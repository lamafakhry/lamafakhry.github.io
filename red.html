Welcome to the Red page, where you can learn all about nutrients found in naturally red foods.

Tomato

Tomatoes are the poster child of healthy red foods. They are perhaps best known for their lycopene content. Lycopene is a phytochemical that is technically part of the carotene family; however, lycopene contains no vitamin A activity.

Cherry

Cherries are not only adorable and delicious in pie, they also contain healthy amounts of vitamin C. If you are worried about pits affecting your dental work, be sure to get yourself a high-quality pitter.
